# Product Guidelines - Hapback

## Tone and Voice
*   **Precise & Engineering-led:** The voice of Hapback is one of technical accuracy and clarity. We don't just "simulate" an iPod; we aim to accurately recreate the mechanical and digital logic of the original hardware.
*   **Authentic Terminology:** We use the exact labels and navigation structures found on the original devices (e.g., "Songs," "Artists," "Photos," "Settings"). This ensures the user's mental model from the past remains perfectly valid.

## UI/UX Principles
*   **Skeuomorphic Precision:** The interface should look and feel like a physical object. This means meticulous attention to lighting, shadows, and textures that define the 6th Generation iPod aesthetic.
*   **Haptic-First Interaction:** Design is centered around the virtual click wheel. Every visual transition and menu scroll should be a direct consequence of the wheel's "rotation" and "clicks."
*   **Stitch-Driven Design:** The UI is developed using Stitch to ensure high-fidelity prototyping and a streamlined path from visual design to implemented SwiftUI views.

## Typography
*   **Historical Accuracy:** Where possible, typography should mirror the Myriad or Chicago fonts used in the target iPod eras to maintain the iconic visual profile.
*   **High Contrast:** Legibility is key, following the high-contrast display patterns of the original LCDs.

## Interaction Design
*   **The "Click" is Central:** No interaction is complete without its corresponding auditory and haptic feedback. The "click" is the heartbeat of the application.
*   **Momentum & Physics:** Menu scrolling and Cover Flow must exhibit authentic physics—momentum, friction, and inertia—to convince the user they are manipulating a physical scroll wheel.
