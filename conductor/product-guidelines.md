# Product Guidelines - Hapback

## Tone and Voice
*   **Precise & Engineering-led:** The voice of Hapback is one of technical accuracy and clarity. We aim to capture the essence of the mechanical and digital logic of the retro music player era.
*   **Intuitive Labels:** We use intuitive labels and navigation structures that are familiar to users of classic portable music players (e.g., "Songs," "Artists," "Settings"). This ensures the user's mental model remains valid.

## UI/UX Principles
*   **Skeuomorphic Precision:** The interface should look and feel like a physical object. This means meticulous attention to lighting, shadows, and textures that define the classic glossy aesthetic.
*   **Haptic-First Interaction:** Design is centered around the virtual click wheel. Every visual transition and menu scroll should be a direct consequence of the wheel's "rotation" and "clicks."
*   **Stitch-Driven Design:** The UI is developed using Stitch to ensure high-fidelity prototyping and a streamlined path from visual design to implemented SwiftUI views.

## Typography
*   **Legible Retro Typography:** Typography should be legible and evoke the feel of early digital displays, prioritizing clarity and high contrast.
*   **High Contrast:** Legibility is key, following the high-contrast display patterns of classic LCDs.

## Interaction Design
*   **The "Click" is Central:** Interaction is enhanced with auditory and haptic feedback. The "click" confirms the user's action.
*   **Fluid Motion:** Menu scrolling and Album Flow should exhibit fluid motion and responsiveness to user input, providing a satisfying navigation experience.
